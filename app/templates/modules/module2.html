Building Credit


What is Credit?

Credit is the ability to borrow money or access goods or services with the understanding that you’ll pay later. A credit score is a numerical rating representing the perceived ability of a person to fulfill their financial commitments, based on an analysis of their credit history and financial circumstances.

Why are they important?

If you have a higher credit score, you can get lower interest rates. Here are some people who care about your credit score:

  •   Creditors/Lenders
    •   For example: Car/house loans
  •   Insurance companies
  •   Landlords
  •   Employers
  •   Utility Companies
  •   Government Agencies
  •   You!



What Factors Make Up Your Credit Score?

  •   Length of history
  •   New Accounts
  •   Amount owed
  •   Types of credit
  •   Payment History

So…How Do You Build Credit?

  1. Pay your bills on time: Late payments can significantly harm your credit score. Always pay your bills on time, and if you can't, contact your creditors to work out a payment plan.

  2. Keep your credit card balances low: High credit card balances can also hurt your credit score. Keep your balances low and pay them off in full each month if possible.

  3. Use credit responsibly: Avoid opening too many credit accounts at once, as this can indicate to lenders that you're taking on too much debt. Also, avoid maxing out your credit cards or using them for unnecessary purchases.
